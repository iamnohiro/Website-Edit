Brazil Launched New Telecom Monitoring

A recent study from MarketsandMarkets entitled "M2M Satellite Communication Market [VSAT, AIS, Satellite Telemetry, Satellite Modems, Gateways, Managed Services, Business Services, and Broadband Services] - Worldwide Market Forecast and Analysis (2014 - 2019)" forecasts that the Machine-to-machine (M2M) market for satellite communication will be worth $4,763.4 million by 2019. It also indicates an increase in data communication need from M2M applications which can possibly open more revenues for the market.

The study focused mostly on technology, services, drivers and retrains, challenges and opportunities of ongoing market trends for M2M in North America, Europe, the Middle East and Africa (MEA), Asia Pacific including Japan and Latin America. Out of all the regions, the largest revenue generation for service in the satellite M2m market comes from North America. While Asia Pacific is still expected to show a very significant rise and revenue expansion due to inter and intra Asian trade among various countries in the region. Apart from these insights, the study also showed the competitive landscape of the market. MarketandMarkets' study provides an in-depth analysis of the technological approaches, as well as marketing strategies that the major players in the market are adopting.

Almost every vertical for the M2M market has adopted the cloud-based platform and this has created million dollar opportunities for solution providers, particularly for managed data center services, broadband services and business services. It has also made quite an enormous presence across several vertical markets like automobiles, transport and logistics, oil and gas, smart metering and utilities, industrial and agricultural services, even healthcare. From among these verticals, Transportation and Logistics has the largest market share and is expected to grown exponentially within the forecast period.

Brazil and other regions in Latin America are adopting the same trends as well. With Brazil opening up new doors for M2M, major players in the region such as Parlacom Brasil are eager to take advantage of these new developments. "The satellite M2M market is indeed a promising market. It provides not just faster data transmission for mission critical tasks and projects but will also yield fast returns in terms of revenue and investments." CEO and Founder of Parlacom Brazil, an affiliate of the LeadingQuest Group of Companies, Clovis Lacerda said.

"I will not be surprised if M2M providers in Latin America, Brazil and other developing countries all over the world will begin to switch to a cloud-based platform. With Parlacom Brasil, we take pride in our cloud-based, fully integrated web-based management tool that enables remote management of systems, service and assets, efficiently and effectively. There are still plenty of opportunities to boost various aspects of the business, such as tapping into the satellite M2M market, and we are definitely considering that. " Lacerda further added.
