Brazil's financial institution to integrate a M2M-enabled financial system

The traditional retail industry has advanced dramatically with the advent of wireless M2M-enabled financial systems, POS terminals and ATM solutions. Using today's POS systems, anyone can instantaneously process payments virtually anywhere inside and outside the four walls of a shop or banking institution. And these transactions are not limited to credit cards and debit cards to checks, smart chip cards and electronic benefits transfers (EBTs). M2M-enabled payment systems are this generation's answers in providing virtually accessible, flexible and more efficient banking and financial services.

In this light, one of the largest financial institutions in Brazil will soon pilot new NCR technology, the global leader in consumer transaction technologies. Bradesco aims to provide consumers a quick means doing account credit for cash deposits that are made through these ATM and M2M-enabled financial service solutions. This new technology which will pilot in June, enables the account holders to make cash deposits without the use of envelopes. This new initiative will make Bradesco one of the first financial institutions in Brazil to integrate a smart deposit system with its ATM channels.

According to Bradesco, both account and non-account holders will benefit from this as the account holder will be able to deposit cash amounts and have them instantly credited into their accounts. The great news is that the technology is made available 24/7 just like automated teller machines are, and transactions can be made even on holidays and weekends. In the same light, non-account holder no longer need to go to a teller to make deposit cash. The ATMs specially developed by NCR for Bradesco have technology that mixes hardware created in Brazil at the company's factory situated within the city of Manaus, State of Amazonas.

The director of Digital Channels at Bradesco, Luca Cavalcanti said, "Putting a stack of cash directly into an ATM, without using envelopes, and crediting the amount online into the account makes sense for clients' lives. And that's our goal: to improve our clients' lives through improved access to their financial needs,"

Bradesco and NCR also are operating to increase real-time deposits to checks. This new model can help to cut back the waste of envelopes employed in ATM deposits, give customers greatly improved access to their funds, and decrease costs. "With NCR's technology, Bradesco takes the lead once again and inaugurates this new service in Brazil. Bradesco is consistently a pioneer in using technology to deliver quality services throughout the country." said Elias Silva, vice president of NCR Latin America and Caribbean.

Parlacom Brasil, one of the leading providers of M2M-enabled financial and POS systems and solutions in the area, believes that point-of-Sale M2M applications are effective means for the consumers to conduct their daily financial transactions, whether they want to make a payment, use automated teller machines or ticketing machines. The retail sector increasingly utilizing wireless and cellular technology, to increase sales deals. Banks on the other hand, use M2M technology in keeping track of wireless and remote financial transactions.

LeadingQuest and Parlacom Brasil Founder and CEO, Clovis Lacerda said, "M2M at point-of-sale is becoming a more common means for financial establishments and retail shops as wireless technology becomes ubiquitous. It can be accustomed to support secure, real time payments and transactions, and provide retailers with better visibility into their inventory with real-time data management."

"The benefits of the M2M-enabled financial systems are obvious. And as we bring the payment process to the customer, efficiency of services greatly improves." Lacerda added.
