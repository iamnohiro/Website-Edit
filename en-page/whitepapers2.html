White Paper: Android Software Development

Android Then and Now

Android is an open-source Linux based application and software platform for mobile devices with Java as its main platform. Android uses java libraries. It was developed by Open Handset Alliance which included nearly 86 software, hardware and telecom companies then. The Open Handset Alliance was led by Google and the initial developer of android was Android Inc., which was also purchased by Google. Today, Android is known as the word's most powerful mobile platform.

There is nothing quite like Android as it continues to power millions of mobile devices and support hundreds of millions of applications in more than 200 countries all over the world., especially now that people are switching from laptops to smart-phones and tablets. Android has the largest installed base of all mobile platforms and is still growing rapidly. It is the only application with more than 700,000 apps released daily.

The Android Market

Android will retain its reign with a 76% forecast market share in 2018 and a CAGR of 10.7% from 2014 to 2018.

Through its partnerships and the contributions of the open-source Linux Community now consisting of 300 hardware, software and telecommunication partners, Android has no doubt become the fastest-growing mobile operating system and has become the favorite of consumers and developers because of its unique openness. Over the years, this has driven demands on app consumption. Thus opening more doors for opportunity in Android Software Development.

Android Software Development

Android application and software development is one of the leading industries in the android market. This market is still growing daily as millions of devices search for newer and better applications that are made available on Google Play. Android application and software development is used and most popular, in developing smart phone and tablet applications. However, recent developments include connected commodity appliances, where apps enable devices to operate independently. Recent development also includes the recently launched Google Wearable.

Why is Android the popular choice?

For one obvious reason - Android is the only platform that offers the most number of applications for free.
And because it is open-source, it gives developers more room to innovate and be more creative. It provides everything that developers need to build the best-in-class experience for the consumers. The Android platform has a simple application model that allows the deployment of applications generally for millions of users across various devices, be it a smart phone, tablet or wearable device.

Android's application framework also enables developers to reuse and replace components. Plus, it offers several features that are not available on other platforms, such as automation, wireless app installation, custom ROMS and true application integration. These are simple perks that add the most value, encouraging more developers all over the world to participate and become a part of this rich ecosystem.

And of course, the Android platform also has other benefits to offer such as:

  • Wide Range of Hardware – Its compatibility with an enormous number of hardware components from various manufacturers opens up unlimited opportunities to develop applications.
  • Better Integration – It focuses on collaboration and is optimized to create the best experience in user interaction through social media integration.
  • Publishing & Reviews – Applications can be readily made available to users through Google Play, without the difficulties of passing app store criteria.
  • Better Analytics – It also provides a breakdown of installs and uninstalls on several different parameters. Google has clearly utilized its knowledge in Analytics into this space and the whole Android application and software development market can leverage this in creating better applications and software.
