White Paper: M2M Messaging At A Glance

Machine-to-Machine (M2M)technology has been around for quite some time. In fact it can be hard to determine when it actually started. Small developments were noted in the early 1990's. However, M2M technology, has rapidly grown over the last decade. M2M Messaging is one of the many M2M services that allows automated machines to communicate and send messages to each other or to other devices.

How Does It Works

M2M messaging uses certain protocols that only machines understand. If two machine are trying to communicate with each other, they need a protocol that both of them understand. Communication between machines used to be very expensive since it required a lot of data to be transmitted over a wireless network.

However with the development of Short Message Service (SMS), this has quickly changed. As the new technology was introduced to the market, further developed and became widely used, wireless network providers had lowered prices for individual machine-to-machine messages. Many M2M providers took advantage of such huge savings and started using SMS to send out messages instead. SMS uses standard communication protocols that allows mobile phones and fixed lines to exchange messages.

While machines can send and receive messages through SMS, it can also include coded instructions that the other machines can interpret. As technology evolves, M2M messaging is no longer constricted to SMS alone. Some M2M messaging solutions utilize another protocol called SMPP, which stands for Short Messaging Peer-to-Peer. It is an open industry standard in telecommunications that provides more flexibility in data transmission. Another protocol used these days is the Adaptive Machine Messaging Protocol (AMMP), aimed at bring the Internet of Things to realistic measures with M2M communication.

The M2M Market

The Machine-to-machine market is exploding and its potential for massive growth is undeniable. In fact, analyst have made various projection on how M2M technology will grow in highly urbanized areas such as the US, Europe, Latin America and as well as in Asia. As per research studies, China is currently leading growth in M2M communication with over 50 million connections.

For decades, M2M industry leaders have been cognizant about the benefits and the advantages that these wireless connections can bring to consumers and businesses alike. A huge number of devices, applications and solutions have been deployed to showcase these benefits. Vertical markets have been growing almost in every sector and more applications have been brought into the picture such as GPS tracking systems, e-Health Services, Smart Metering & Utilities, and Smart Grids among many others.

A recent forecast reveals that there will be over 50 billion connected devices in 2020. And the global M2M services market will grow at a CAGR of 21.35% from 2013 to 2018. One of the key factors that analysts note is the increasing adoption of M2M at a small medium-size business to the enterprise level. And if the number of devices will be equated to the world's population, each person in the planet will have at least 10 devices connected through this technology. With recent reports showing that mobile devices and SIM cards outnumbering humans, this projection is not entirely unrealistic given the growth rate of the market.
